#1 Circuits that have been the scene of the most accidents in Formula 1

While F1 cars were rapidly evolving their performance and speed, circuit safety did not always keep pace. Since the 1950s, when the category was created, more than 40 drivers have died in races and tests. Currently, the sport has adopted various safety procedures both in cars and on circuits, which has greatly reduced the number of fatalities. Next to it, we have a graph that shows the ranking of circuits that have been the scenes of accidents during the years 1950 - 2018.

#2 Nationality of drivers who reached the highest speeds in a single lap in F1

It's from Brazil! Brazilian driver Rubens Barrichelo has the highest speed reached in a single lap at 257.32 km/h. Followed by German driver Michael Schumacher, who reached a total speed of 256.324 km/h.

#3 Most victorious drivers in Formula 1 history

So far, the most victorious driver in Formula 1 history has been Michael Schumacher, with 91 victories. He is no longer active and is likely to be surpassed soon by the second place, Lewis Hamilton, who has 62 victories.

#4 What is the average top speed of the first places in their fastest laps?

The average of the highest speeds in the fastest laps of F1 is 211 km/h. This incredible speed can be achieved by drivers because their cars are extremely lightweight.
